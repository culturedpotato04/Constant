The Chapters That Made Us

Some moments refuse to become memories, they stay alive

June 24, 2022 · After 12:55 PM

The Smile That Rewrote Everything

A pale pink kurti. Tired eyes still recovering from covid. And a formal smile that had no idea what it was about to start. I remember thinking how someone could look that gentle after going through so much. That afternoon, without knowing it, something inside me quietly decided: this softness needed protecting. This person needed to keep smiling. Whatever it takes. I walked in as a stranger that day. I never quite walked out the same.

September 2022 · One Late Night

When Silence Spoke Louder

That night had no plan. Just us talking past midnight, peeling back layers we didn't usually show anyone. I told you things I'd kept locked away. You didn't judge, didn't fix, didn't run. You just listened. And then, without a single word of explanation, you sent a forehead kiss gif. That was your way of saying, "I've got you." No grand declaration, no dramatic confession. Just warmth, delivered at 2 AM, through a tiny animated gif that somehow carried more weight than any sentence ever could.

December 14, 2024

When Pixels Became Real

Two and a half years of screens, voice notes, and goodnight texts finally collapsed into real footsteps. I brought you to my college, showed you the corridors I walk through, the bench I sit on when I'm thinking about you, the canteen where I imagine you sitting across from me. Every spot I'd described in texts suddenly had you standing in it. The distance didn't disappear that day. It just stopped mattering.

June 24, 2026

Still Choosing You

Four years. Not always smooth, not always easy, but always you. Through rough patches that tested patience, through silences that felt heavier than arguments, through late night calls that healed more than either of us admitted. You're still the first person I want to tell things to. Still the voice I want to hear at the end of a long day. Still the notification that makes me pause everything. Four years later, nothing about choosing you feels like a choice anymore. It just feels like breathing.
